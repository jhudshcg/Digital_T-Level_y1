Part 2: Root Cause Analysis (1.3.4)

When systems fail, we use specific frameworks to find the truth.

A. The "5 Whys" Technique

Purpose: To move past symptoms (what happened) to the root cause (why it happened).

WORKED EXAMPLE: LATE FOR WORK
1. Why was I late? My car wouldn't start. (Symptom)
2. Why wouldn't it start? The battery was dead.
3. Why was the battery dead? The alternator is broken.
4. Why is the alternator broken? The service belt snapped.
5. ROOT CAUSE: I didn't replace the service belt at the recommended mileage. (Human Error / Maintenance)

Part 3: The Problem Solving Lifecycle (1.3.5)

There are 6 standard steps to solving high-level problems.

INCIDENT LOG: "THE WI-FI IS SLOW"

12:00 PM: Users in Room 304 reported slow internet. (The issue starts)

12:10 PM: We checked router logs, ran speed tests, and counted connected devices.

12:30 PM: We compared the data. Logs showed 50 users, but the router maxes out at 30. The router is overloaded.

12:45 PM: We decided to install a second router to balance the load.

2:00 PM: Technician installed the new hardware.

Next Day: We tested the speed again in Room 304. It is fixed.
